Equations: Factor, Term, and Expression

Factor

In the PL/0 language, a Factor refers to either an identifier that has been declared (as a constant or variable), number, or an expression is that is between parentheses. This is would be used constantly by other grammar in the PL/0 compiler.

Term

A Term is when a Factor grammar is followed by either a multiplication or division sign and again by another Factor. The best way to describe Term is that it looks like a multiplication or division equation. The math formula does need to have only one multiplication/division sign, it can be followed by a pattern that consists of a multiplication/division sign and a Factor (numerous of times).

For instance, a term can be

  • X *2
  • 7 / 8
  • Y * 2 / 5 * x * 7 / 1

Expression

In the PL/0, an Expression is equal to what a Term is defined to be; however, it can include an addition or subtraction sign beforehand (if the sign is a subtraction, the PL/0 will consider it as a negation). There can also can be a continuous pattern following Term which includes an addition/subtraction sign and Term.
